Failure shape

Mean vs mode of over%

Chronic but mild misses

Onboarding Import SRP: fail rate 59%, but over% mode is only 5.3% and mean 16.3%. Gates are scraped often by a little — not catastrophic latency spikes.

Outlier-heavy fails

Unlock after import: mean over% 73% vs mode 4.2%. Most fails barely miss; a long tail of bad sessions inflates the mean.

Consistently slow when it fails

Account creation: mode 77.7% and median 95.8% over a 3300ms gate. When it fails, it usually fails by nearly 2× — a product/perf issue, not noise.

How to read over%

Mode ≈ typical failure magnitude.
Mean ≈ pulled up by outliers.

High mean + low mode ⇒ flaky infra / device spikes.
High mean + high mode ⇒ the gate is systematically too tight for current builds.

Next steps

Takeaways

1. Prioritize onboarding import + account creation

Highest fail rates on main. Account creation overshoots by ~2× when it fails — investigate token-list render path after new account.

2. Unlock after import needs both perf work and outlier triage

25% fail against 2200ms. Mode is tiny (4%); separate “near miss” vs “>7s outlier” cohorts before moving the gate.

3. Uniswap interaction is expensive when it trips

22.5% fail with thr 24200ms and mean over% 170%. Functional timeouts and QG misses both show up here.

4. Keep launch baselines as the north star

Cold→Onboarding, Asset View, Aggregated Balance, Predict Available Balance prove the gate framework can stay green.

5. Watch Seedless / Perps / Predict deposit

Mid-pack fail rates (6–14%). Not urgent like top offenders, but enough volume to catch regressions early.
